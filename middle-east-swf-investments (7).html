Strategic trends & analysis
AI infrastructure as the defining thesis
Over 60% of tracked deal value flows into AI — frontier models, data centers, and chip design. MGX alone committed north of $85B across Aligned Data Centers, Stargate, OpenAI, and its France campus. This is not passive exposure; it is an active industrial policy bet that AI infrastructure leadership confers geopolitical leverage.
Co-investment as a coordination mechanism
Eleven deals involved multiple MENA SWFs, spanning frontier AI (xAI, Anthropic, Databricks), infrastructure (AIP, BAIIF) and now media (Warner Bros. Discovery). The Paramount/WBD deal is the first three-way Gulf SWF co-investment — PIF, QIA and newly-launched L'imad collectively committing ~$24B. QIA and MGX remain the most active AI co-investing pair.
Mega take-privates redefine PIF's global footprint
PIF's $55B Electronic Arts consortium is the largest all-cash take-private in history, pushing estimated annual deployment to $36B — an 81% year-on-year surge. Combined with Heathrow and Selfridges, PIF is building permanent ownership in global entertainment, travel, and retail infrastructure.
Mubadala diversifies across the full deal spectrum
From an $8.7B wealth management take-private (CI Financial) to a $150M seed-adjacent biotech portfolio and a ground-up pharma company, Mubadala is the most structurally diversified fund in this dataset. Its $1.38B Crusoe lead is a rare instance of an SWF leading a Series E in a capital-intensive compute business.
Energy transition as a strategic hedge
Deals in critical minerals (TechMet, Ivanhoe Mines), green energy (Greenko, Techem), and copper mining reflect a deliberate hedge against hydrocarbon dependency. QIA's $500M Ivanhoe stake and ADIA's Greenko position target supply chains underpinning the very clean energy transition these funds are financing elsewhere.
US dominates; Europe rising fast
Over 55% of deals by value target US-headquartered companies, led by AI and financial services. European deal value is surging — Techem ($7.9B), Apleona ($4.2B), BASF Coatings ($8.6B), and MGX's France data campus signal a deliberate push to build strategic presence ahead of EU AI regulation.
